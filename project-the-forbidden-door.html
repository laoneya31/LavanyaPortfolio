The Forbidden Door

Process

I set out to create a self-directed dark fiction comic retelling Charles Perrault's Bluebeard in a modern cinematic setting. The goal was to take a classical narrative and reimagine it entirely through original photography, editing, and layout, rather than relying on conventional illustration, while keeping a consistent dark, atmospheric tone across every page.

Every element of this project was conceived and executed independently. I wrote the original script and produced initial sketches and storyboards before shooting, then personally cast, directed, and photographed all of the subjects used in the comic. From there, I edited every image in Photoshop, cartoonifying real photographs and manipulating lighting and color to build atmospheric backgrounds, before moving into Figma to arrange panels, control pacing, and add typography. I treated layout as an active storytelling tool rather than a container, making deliberate choices about panel size and composition to control narrative rhythm and emphasize key story moments. The steepest learning curve was managing layers across both Photoshop and Figma simultaneously while keeping a consistent visual tone throughout. Directing and photographing real people added another layer of complexity, since shots had to be carefully planned in advance to ensure the edited results matched the comic's intended cinematic aesthetic. Working with varied panel types and frame compositions, unfamiliar territory for me, required considerable experimentation and revision before the choices felt intentional rather than arbitrary.

The final outcome is a ready, continuous-scroll comic with a cohesive dark aesthetic, original photography, and a fully realized narrative arc, produced entirely independently from first concept through to final layout. This project speaks to my ability to direct a creative vision across multiple disciplines, photography, image editing, and composition, and to treat visual structure as a deliberate narrative device rather than an afterthought.
