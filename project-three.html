Process

I wanted to build a narrative platformer that translated an emotional experience into interactive mechanics rather than just decoration. The brief I set for myself was to design a fully playable five-level 2D platformer in Java, where every level, enemy, and power-up functioned as a metaphor for a phase of long-distance love. The challenge was making an abstract emotional arc legible through level design, from the gentle Forest of Memories to the disorienting Mirror World, while still delivering a complete, functional game within a tight production timeline.

I worked solo across every stage of development, from concept to final build. I started by writing the original narrative and planning the class architecture with a UML diagram before touching code, which let me think through the systems logically rather than improvising structure later. I built the full game in Java, implementing a progressive mechanic system that introduced new player abilities across levels so difficulty scaled in step with the story. Each of the five realms was designed with distinct enemies, hazards, and environmental storytelling, and I created hand-drawn storyboards and level sketches upfront to plan pacing before building anything in code. The biggest challenge was delivering five fully functional levels within a tight time constraint while bugs compounded as mechanics grew more complex. I solved this by prioritizing a clear feature hierarchy, locking down core movement and collision before layering in power-ups and enemies, and tackling bugs systematically level by level instead of trying to fix everything at once.

The result is a complete, playable narrative platformer with five levels, a full enemy system, progressive power-ups, original artwork, and synthesized audio, delivered solo within the project timeline. This project demonstrates my ability to carry a project independently from architectural planning through implementation, and to use structured, systems-level thinking to manage complexity without losing sight of the creative intent driving the work.
